--- layout: null section-type: social title: Main Social Phenomenas --- ## Major social phenomenas This part was done by Sina. How did the RTS coverage of important social phenomena like the sexual revolution, women's voting right, and terrorism evolved over time, using the number of occurences and co-occurences. Note: Go over the bar plots to see appearing the co-occurenes for a given year. What we mean by co-occurece is : words that have appeared the most with the chosen topic for a given year.
In some years, there is no dominant co-occurence (lower that the set threshold) so you don't see any thing. We have chosen to study some of the major social phenomenas of the 20th century.
Looking at the various plots below, we can see some trends appearing :
For women's voting right, it peaks at 1971 when there was the election in Switzerland. Then it's much less talked about, with some exceptions, the last one being in 2017 and women's voting right attributed to Saudi women.
For Homosexuality, we can observe that there was absolutely nothing about the subject until 1960! It picks at 2003. Observe that in the first apperences, the dominant co-occurences are films (movies), livre (books) showing that artists were trying to talk about the project. Whereas in the last years, although the number of occurence has not changed (even decreased over last five years), but, the dominant co-occurences are marriage, children and medicine showing the progress made on the subject. Finally, for terrorisme, it's interesting to see that after every pic, there is an important relax in the occurence, on the following year.
